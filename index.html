Project Description

Goals

The overall objective of this project is to derive compact and robust neural networks for visual detection in autonomous driving cars. Neural network efficiency and robustness are our two major goals. To be more specific,

  • #1: We propose to achieve the efficiency goal via neural network compression (e.g., pruning in a way that is proactive and aware of both location and class separability).
  • #2: For the robustness goal, we will first analyze the robustness of compressed visual detectors for self-driving before improving the robustness using adversarial training based approaches.

Intellectual Merit

The proposed research is expected to contribute a deep network pruning framework for visual detection in self-driving vehicles that can greatly boost model efficiency via trimming away features of low utility to the task. More specifically, (1) the resulting specialist networks will be small enough to fit onto commodity cars and can be updated instantly via a 5G or even 4G connection. (2) In addition, the small models will require less computation (e.g., < 5B FLOPs) and lower latency (e.g., < 10 ms), which can translate to low energy consumption and high survival rates during emergencies. (3) The task-specific pruning will potentially reduce overfitted and interfering features, leading directly or indirectly to improved model robustness. Therefore, the outcomes are expected to greatly advance the field and lay the foundation for our further research towards fully autonomous driving (Level 5). Also, this project is expected to reveal the potential of and bring more attention to the less explored specialist path to deep detection model design.

Broader Impacts

The project’s resulting compact models will consume less energy and generate less carbon dioxide, contributing to a better environment. Through facilitating a wider deployment of self-driving cars, this proposed project will also help reduce driving-related labor costs and provide greater mobility to more individuals, especially the elderly and the disabled. In addition, the PI will involve a few grad and undergrad students in this research. Special consideration will be given to students from underrepresented groups. Finally, the PI will introduce relevant technology and findings from the project to the classroom to educate more students. This will prepare them for tomorrow’s automobile industry in Ohio and nearby Michigan, top two states for auto production in the US.
